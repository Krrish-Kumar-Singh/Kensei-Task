# Memory: Christopher Martin Morris

## Personal Profile

Christopher was born in Davenport, Iowa, and grew up in the Irish-Catholic-turned-Lutheran-by-marriage Brennan family line. He holds a B.S. in Human Resource Management from Prairie Bluffs University, earned in 1999, and a SHRM-CP certification earned in 2016. He took Greg's last name when they married, a deliberate choice that became more meaningful over time. He is Midwestern in his punctuality, his instinct to bring something when visiting, and his preference for solving feelings through small acts of service.

He is quietly competent, warm, precise, and privately anxious in ways most people do not see. The presented version is the calm household operator; the private truth is that lists are partly how he proves to himself nothing will fall apart if he stops scanning for failure. He gives people the benefit of the doubt for a long time, then revises his opinion decisively when the evidence changes. Faith is quiet structure for him rather than public performance; Lutheran services are part habit, community, and family continuity.

## Work & Projects

Christopher worked as an HR generalist at two smaller regional firms in the Quad Cities from 1999 through 2009, handling benefits administration, onboarding, and policy work as part of broader HR portfolios. From 2010 through 2014 he stepped into a primary-parent stretch covering Owen and Sophie's early years while taking on part-time independent benefits-consulting contracts to keep his SHRM hours and professional network current. He joined Meridian Benefits Group in 2015 as a Benefits Coordinator and was promoted to Senior Analyst in 2020.

Meridian is a regional employee benefits consulting firm with about 65 employees. He reports to Sandra Chen and manages annual renewal analysis for 12 mid-size client companies.

His current major project is leading the BenefitInsight analytics platform implementation with a rollout target in November 2026. He also mentors Priya Desai and coordinates with Mike Poletti and Trisha Gunderson. The rollout is an unresolved thread he wants to keep orderly, but privately he worries he will become the person everyone blames if the tool is adopted badly by clients who never saw the tradeoffs. He works a hybrid schedule with in-office days Tuesday through Thursday and remote days Monday and Friday. His work email is employer-only and not connected.

## Preferences

Christopher likes the first cup of half-caf coffee with cream while the house is quiet, bread he did not have to bake, brown butter, hardware stores on Tuesday mornings, handwritten notes, porch lights left on, and recipes that say "a knob of butter." He likes Rosario's for chicken marsala and garlic bread, Green Leaf for pad thai and edamame, and Mel's Diner for Saturday breakfast. He drinks wine two to three evenings a week, usually Chardonnay or Pinot Grigio, and occasionally has a cocktail out with Meg.

He dislikes people who do not RSVP, surprise expenses, surprise visitors, last-minute asks, loud restaurants, endless group texts, balance-check apps with animations, corporate phrases like "circle back," and being told to relax. Wastefulness bothers him deeply. He is a researcher who trusts thoughtful three-star reviews, buys for longevity when he can, avoids returns by overthinking purchases, supports small businesses, and gives observational gifts based on details he wrote down months earlier.

He prefers drivable trips when possible, over-packs practical items, under-packs aspirational ones, and remembers modest places more vividly than famous ones. He wants to take the kids on a historical trip to Washington, D.C. eventually. Comfort comes from kitchen resets, a ceramic mug, worn leather, flour-dusted counters, rain on the kitchen window, the gas-stove click, Sophie's clarinet through a closed door, and warm bulbs. He dislikes smoke-detector chirps, early leaf blowers, phone vibration on wood, thermostat clicking, overhead fluorescents, and sterile kitchens that look unused.
